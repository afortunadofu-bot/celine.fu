How I Think我如何思考問題

Every problem is a structure
waiting to be found. 每個問題都是一個
等待被找到的結構。

01

Define before you dive先定義，再深入

The most expensive mistake in analytics is answering the wrong question precisely. Before any analysis, I ask: what decision does this need to support? What does "good enough" look like here? I frame the problem before I touch the data.分析工作中最昂貴的錯誤，是精準地回答了錯誤的問題。在任何分析前，我先問：這份分析要支援什麼決策？「夠好」在這個情境下是什麼樣子？我先框定問題，再碰數據。

02

Break into smallest testable unit拆解到最小可測試單元

Complexity isn't a wall — it's a stack of simpler problems. I decompose ambiguous tasks (e.g., "why did revenue drop?") into mutually exclusive layers: volume vs. price vs. mix vs. timing vs. reporting error. This prevents confirmation bias and finds the real driver faster.複雜性不是一堵牆，而是一疊更簡單的問題。我將模糊任務（例如「為什麼收入下降？」）拆解為互斥層次：量 vs. 價 vs. 結構 vs. 時序 vs. 報表錯誤。防止確認偏誤，更快找到真正驅動因素。

03

Translate findings into action將洞察轉化為行動

Analysis that doesn't change behavior is just entertainment. Every deliverable I produce — financial dashboard, white paper, or variance report — ends with: "Here's what this means, and here's what I recommend next." Insight without direction is incomplete.不改變行為的分析只是娛樂。我產出的每份交付物——財務儀表板、白皮書或 variance 報告——都以「這代表什麼，以及我建議下一步怎麼做」作為結尾。沒有方向的洞察是不完整的。
